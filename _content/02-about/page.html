I have always enjoyed using computers and the internet. I fondly remember the many evenings spent playing point and click games on floppy disks, listening to the shrieking of a 56k modem, and patiently waiting for website content to load bit by bit by bit...

I had my first peek behind the scenes of making websites whilst working for the great charity SPANA as a fundraiser. I was hooked straight away and decided that this was what I wanted to do for a living.

So, in 2009, I completed a course at the London College to get to grips with HTML, CSS and some basic PHP.

Since then I have been involved in more than 30 projects and have worked with great organisations, designers and developers.

I try to be conscious about the consequences of my actions and the impact it has on people, other creatures and the environment. I want to do my bit by focusing on working for good causes wherever I can, follow ethical guidelines and offer a 10% discount on work for non-profit organisations.
